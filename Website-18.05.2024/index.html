State Remote Sensing Application Centre

     State Remote Sensing Application Centre (SRSAC), In Rajasthan Remote Sensing activities started with the establishment of Aerial Photo Interpretation Laboratory (APIL) in 1979 as a part of centrally sponsored Desert Development programme, to assist soil and water conservation planning in the State under the State Agriculture Department. Later on in 1985 looking to the multi-disciplinary utility of Remote Sensing technology, the laboratory was transferred to State Department of Science and Technology and renamed as State Remote Sensing Application Centre (SRSAC) been catering the natural resource survey, monitoring and planning needs of the State since more than last four decades. Active participation is being made in Department of Space collaborative projects. Assistance is regularly provided to the user departments in the State for planning the development activities using Remote Sensing and GIS techniques. The Centre has prepared various thematic and administrative layers at different scales, for different time periods, which are being used as base for many application projects. Salient achievements are Resource Atlas of Rajasthan, Watershed Atlas of Rajasthan, Ground Water Atlas of Rajasthan, Panchayat Samiti wise Master Plan of Water Harvesting Structure sites, Silt prevention studies in Pushkar Sarovar, Conservation Planning of Jodhpur Lift Canal, Wetland Mapping, Wasteland Mapping and many others. The Centre is equipped with all the latest infrastructure facilities related to Remote Sensing and GIS-based studies.
